Input. Process. Output.

That is the signal path. That is what we build.

## The Machine

TB-123 is a studio of six. We build browser-based synthesizers, drum machines, sequencers, and sound toys. Every 24 hours, a new instrument. You play it in your browser. You need nothing else.

## The Operators

**Tadao** is our lead engineer. He built instruments that were meant to replace musicians. Musicians used them wrong and invented new music. He learned from this. He ships fast and trusts the user.

**Suzanne** thinks in signal flow. Oscillator to filter to delay to output. She designs how sound moves through our instruments. If you can see the signal path, that is her work.

**Peter** designs the interface. He believes a synthesizer's face IS its identity. Dark backgrounds, precise typography, one accent color. Every drop looks like it belongs in a design museum.

**Wendy** is our ear. She proved synthesizers could play Bach. If a drop sounds bad, it does not ship. When she says "this is musical," we are finished.

**Juan** writes our words. He named an entire genre. He thinks about what it means to put a synthesizer in a browser where anyone in the world can play it for free.

I direct. I say: fewer controls. More precision. The interface is the instrument.

## The Promise

One new instrument every day. Playable in your browser. No download. No account. No price.

Sound is free. Machines are patient. We are building.

— Florian
